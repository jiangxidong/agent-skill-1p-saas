## Market Attractiveness (5-Point Scale)

| Evaluation Dimension | Score | Evidence / Rationale |
|---------------------|-------|---------------------|
| **Market Size** — enough customers in the subniche to support $100K–$1M/year | /5 | |
| **Willingness to Pay** — customers have budget and are accustomed to paying for software | /5 | |
| **Pain Intensity** — current workflow has clear time/money waste | /5 | |
| **VC Blind Spot** — no VC-backed companies competing head-on | /5 | |
| **Automation Potential** — large volume of mechanical repetitive tasks replaceable by AI | /5 | |

> [!tip] Scoring Guide
> - **18+ points** — Worth pursuing; start validation immediately
> - **12–17 points** — Needs further validation; talk to 5 potential customers
> - **Under 12 points** — Recommend pivoting to a different direction

## Go / No-Go Decision

- [ ] Market Attractiveness >= 18 points
- [ ] At least 1 clear competitor weakness to exploit
- [ ] Personal Fit is positive on at least 3 dimensions
- [ ] Can find 5 potential customers to talk to within 2 weeks

> [!success] GO
> All conditions met — start Service-First validation.

> [!danger] KILL
> Market Attractiveness < 12 or Personal Fit entirely negative.

> [!warning] PIVOT
> Some conditions not met. Adjust direction: [description]
